History

Fortnite has a fascinating history, evolving from a cooperative survival game to a global phenomenon in the Battle Royale genre. When Fortnite's OG season launched, it broke records with 44.7 million players on its biggest day. Over the course of the month, the nostalgia-driven season saw millions of players returning to experience the original map and gameplay elements from Chapter 1.

Minecraft, launched in 2011, revolutionized sandbox gaming with its open-ended gameplay and blocky aesthetics. It has become one of the best-selling video games of all time, with over 200 million copies sold globally. Players have created astonishing builds, from complex redstone machinery to recreations of entire cities, showcasing boundless creativity.

Call of Duty: Black Ops is historical for its intense Cold War setting, blending real-world events like the Bay of Pigs invasion and covert CIA operations with a gripping fictional narrative about espionage, sleeper agents, and psychological warfare. Its portrayal of the era, combined with innovative gameplay and multiplayer features, made it a standout in both storytelling and gaming history.

Grand Theft Auto V (GTA 5) is historical for its innovative multi-protagonist system, expansive open-world design inspired by Los Angeles, and its status as one of the best-selling games of all time, setting new standards in realism and interactive storytelling.
